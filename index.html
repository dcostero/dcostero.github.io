ABOUT ME

WHO AM I?

Hello! My name is Daniel Costero and I am currently a quant at Arfima. I started my career studying Aerospace Engineering at the Technical University of Madrid (UPM) because I wanted to become an astronaut and travel to the Moon. It was a very challenging degree, but it taught me a lot, especially how to deal with very complex problems. During this time, I discovered my passion for Computational Fluid Dynamics, realizing how effectively we can simulate very complex flows using just a computer. I also faced the inherent difficulties and challenges, which pushed me to pursue a master’s degree specialized in Aerospace Propulsion.

I was fortunate enough to win a scholarship for a double-master program between UPM and ISAE-ENSMA in Poitiers, France, where I specialized in the numerical simulation of combustion processes. This experience deepened my understanding of programming, numerical methods, and statistics, and allowed me to collaborate with some of the leading researchers in this field. However, craving novel work and new challenges, I decided to embark on a PhD.

Thanks to a prestigious Marie-Curie scholarship, I joined a PhD program at the Aerospace department of Politecnico di Milano, one of the top engineering universities globally. My research focused on the numerical simulation of the combustion inside Internal Combustion Engines—arguably the most challenging problem in fluid dynamics due to the complexities of liquid spray injection and turbulent combustion in a moving domain. I developed new numerical schemes to manage mesh motion and topology changes, achieving second-order temporal accuracy.

After finishing my PhD, I was eager for new challenges and wanted to step out of my comfort zone, which led me to start as a Quantitative Researcher at Arfima. Currently, I am working on several projects related to developing a platform to compute the financial valuation of Physical Climate Risk. I am involved in the entire process, from data handling and cloud storage of climate data to the financial models used to price the different products, including all the backend code needed to build the platform. I am learning new things every day and enjoying every minute of it!

My proffesional interests are focused on developing new code to solve interesting and challenging problems. I am particularly passionate about data science and finance and am eager to explore these fields more deeply, ideally simultaneously. I thrive on multidisciplinary problems that demand varied approaches and enjoy collaborating with a team of individuals from diverse backgrounds.

Experience

Profesional Experience

Quant Researcher at Arfima Financial Solutions (Madrid, Spain) August 2023-Now

We are currently developing a platform to measure Physical Climate Risk of a portfolio. The goal is to provide the client a financial valuation of the expected losses of a portfolio on a future date under several climate scenarios. To do so, we collaborate with OS-Climate, an open source project lead by the linux foundation, and whose public repositories can be found here. From there, we are building our own platform where the user can upload the portfolio and evaluate the physical risk that each asset faces and how it will change in the future.

In addition to the open source part, we are developing a proprietary financial library that is able to provide a monetary value of the expected loss of each asset individually and of the complete portfolio. We use models extracted from catastrophe modelling to provide metrics such as PD, LGD, EAD, VaR, Climate VaR or Expected Shortfall among others. These models constitute an extension of pyPricing, a library developed in-house to price different financial products.

As a backend, we use an online storage service where we upload our own hazard datasets as well as all the financial data required. It is optimized to handle large structured databases that are accessed with SQL queries and parallelized to run simulations on several nodes if required. We also use Grafana to create a dashboard where the results are displayed.

PhD student at Politecnico di Milano (Milano, Italy) 2020-2023

I won a Marie-Curie scholarship to study a PhD in Aerospace engineering as part of the EDEM project. The EDEM project is made of 15 PhD students working together with the aim to improve the understanding and design of Dual-Fuel Internal Combustion Engines. This is a novel type of engine that burns 2 different types of fuels simultaneously and is particularly suited for heavy duty machinery and naval applications.

My PhD thesis is entitled "Development of Time-Accurate CFD Methodologies for Dynamic Mesh Handling in Reactive Flow Computations" and you can find it here. I worked on the following topics:

  • Development of a new methodology to use second-order accurate temporal schemes in the presence of topology changes in the computational mesh.
  • Implementation and validation of a hybrid spray model, suited for the simulation of dual-fuel injection.
  • Development of a methodology to couple dynamic overset grids with a Lagrangian spray solver, in order to improve the accuracy in deforming domains.
  • All my work was implemented as an extension of OpenFOAM.
  • I also presented my work in the Parallel Computing Conference in Turin and published a peer reviewed paper, that you can find here.

As part of my PhD, I spent half of the time working at AVL in Graz, Austria, in the Advanced Science and Technology department, collaborating in the implementation of new methodologies for dynamic mesh handling in their software AVL FIRE
